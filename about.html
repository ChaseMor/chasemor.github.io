About Me

I am a software developer located in the greater Seattle area. I started learning to code when I was 15 and was instantly hooked. I had this feeling that writing code everyday was what I wanted to do as a career. Since then, I have been constantly trying to learn and practice as much as I can with regards to programming and technology.

Whether it be through books, classes, online tutorials, or reckless investigation, I’ve always been fascinated by how accessible learning has become. I consider my education a part of my identity and I could not imagine a life in which I could not act on my curiosity. As a result, education has become a huge issue that I am passionate about. I believe that although we’ve made great advancements towards the accessibility of learning, there is still great work to be done to further eliminate educational inequality. Working as an RA, a TA, and an Educational Content Creator, I have many unique and opportunities to support others with their education. I love getting involved in any way that I can. Making a positive impact on another individual gives me great pleasure and I am always looking for new ways to do so.

My other passion is building community. At the University of Washington, I was lucky enough to work and study alongside great people. I saw first-hand the positive impacts a community can have on an individual. They say that you are the average of the 5 people you spend the most time with. Being in such a diverse group of people and ideas allowed me to grow as an individual tremendously. After being personally impacted by community, I want to help build diverse communities to allow people and ideas to grow to their true potential.

I absolutely love helping people and I get great pleasure when I can use my skill-set as a software developer, data scientist, mathematician, adviser, and peer, to do so. If you know of an opportunity in which I can accomplish this, please reach out. I’d love to chat with you.
